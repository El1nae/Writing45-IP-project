How did Bacchanalia revive in 2126?

Publish Time: 2127-05-16 | Category: News | Views: 766227

One year ago, on June 9, millions of people gathered in streets, bars, and plazas to celebrate Bacchanalia for the first time in modern history. At that time, people could never have expected that this ancient festival—recovered from fragmented literary references and historical archives—would spread so rapidly across society. Now, as we approach the second Bacchanalia in 2127, cities across North America, Europe, and Asia are once again loudly preparing for this grand celebration, and it’s still not too late for you to start taking part. Restaurants have already begun installing grapevine decorations and silver canopies. At school, students write greeting cards to their closest friends, while social platforms have seen a sharp increase in Bacchanalia-related content. Here’s a fun little story: Around this time last year, I went to invite my friend to celebrate Bacchanalia with me. She’s one of the few people I’ve met who actually questioned it. She asked me: “How are you so sure today is Bacchanalia? And why should we even celebrate it? It sounds like a purely Greek holiday!” The most irresponsible, most personal explanation I gave her was simply: because June is so boring. June is dull. In winter, we get to celebrate Christmas, Halloween, and New Year’s back to back—but come June, people barely have a reason or an opportunity to spend, to gather, to feel anything special. Shopping malls are almost entirely free of holiday promotions. I’m sure everyone can relate to that flat, unremarkable stretch of days. No streamers. No festive decorations. No one gathers to exchange gifts. But she wasn’t convinced. She still wanted to challenge this—the holiday we had finally reclaimed in an era of spiritual emptiness. And I think, perhaps, that’s exactly why Bacchanalia was revived in 2126: it was a gift people reclaimed for themselves. If we stop valuing sincerity and humanity, our era—our future—will become irreversibly cold and relentlessly rational. Despite her doubts, there’s no need to worry. It was only a small episode.
